Maintenance Recommendations

1. Check Tire Pressure Regularly

Maintain the recommended tire pressure to ensure safety, better fuel efficiency, and longer tire life. Check at least once a week or before long trips.

2. Inspect for Damage

Regularly examine your tires for cracks, cuts, punctures, or bulges. If any damage is detected, have the tire repaired or replaced immediately.

3. Monitor Tread Depth

Ensure your tire tread is not too worn out. Low tread depth reduces grip, especially on wet roads, increasing the risk of accidents.

4. Rotate Tires Periodically

Rotate your tires every 5,000-10,000 km to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan.

5. Keep Tires Properly Aligned

Wheel misalignment can cause uneven tire wear and poor handling. Have your alignment checked if the vehicle pulls to one side.

6. Balance Your Tires

Proper tire balancing ensures a smooth ride and prevents uneven wear. Do this whenever you install or rotate tires.

7. Avoid Overloading

Do not exceed the recommended load capacity of your vehicle. Overloading puts excessive pressure on tires and can lead to damage or blowouts.

8. Drive Carefully

Avoid sudden braking, sharp turns, and hitting potholes. Smooth driving helps extend tire life.
